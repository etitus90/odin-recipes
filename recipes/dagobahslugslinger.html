Dagobah Slug Syrup

"As you probably guessed from its name, this particular cocktail derives its inimitable herbal nip from the sticky slime of the Dagobah swamp slug - or, at least, from the subtitute Dagobah Slug Syrup on this site's first batch of recipes. Despite its decidedly floral notes, the Dagobah Slug Slinger isn't nearly as swampy as you might expect from a drink that originated on Dagobah. That's because it's got a tart citrus base that provides the ideal balance to the slug's sweet, herbaceous ooze. The strong flavors do a nice job of smoothin' out the equally strong portion of booze it contains. But don't be fooled - this one has more kick to it than a master of Teräs Käsi!"

Ingredients

  • 1/2 ounce lime juice
  • 2 ounces orange juice
  • 1 once Dagobah Slug Syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon blue curaçao
  • 2 ounces tequila
  • 1 ounce water
  • Sprig of rosemary for garnishing

Steps

  1. Combine the lime juice, orange juice, Dagobah Slug Syrup, curaçao, tequila, and water in a shaker and give it a few good shakes to combine.
  2. Pour into a tall glass with a few ice cubes and garnish with a sprig of rosemary.
  3. For added flair, light part of the rosemary sprig on fire, then blow out and let smolder.
